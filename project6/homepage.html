Los Angeles Street Art

Along with the glamor and shine of Hollywood, Los Angeles is the cornerstone of the history of street art. These painting and murals reflect the different neighborhoods, people, and culture LA has to offer. Many artists use their gift to express themselves and portray what is happening in the city and around the world. What makes street art so special is the freedom it allots artists to show and say exactly how they feel. There is beauty and creativity all around Los Angeles all you have to do is walk outside…



“THIS IS PRECISELY THE TIME WHEN ARTIST GO TO WORK. THERE IS NO TIME FOR DISPARE, NO PLACE FOR SELF-PITY, NO NEED FOR SILENCE, NO ROOM FOR FEAR. WE SPEAK, WE WRITE, WE DO LANGUAGE. THAT IS HOW CIVILIZATIONS HEAL” -Toni Morrison



8401 W 3rd St, Los Angeles 90048, another at 453 Colyton Street

This piece is part of the Global Angel Wings project created by Colette Miller. It is a life size, interactive art display around the streets on Los Angeles. The meaning behind the wings is to “remind humanity that we are the angels of the earth.” The project has grown and expanded globally but its original purpose to bring people from all backgrounds together and show the goodness in humanity has remained the same.
